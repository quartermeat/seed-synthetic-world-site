Hex Globe

Simulation Sandbox  ·  Touch-First  ·  Android

A pocket-sized spinning globe.
Each hex, a world waiting to happen.

Right now it's a Goldberg polyhedron — 642 hex cells wrapped around a sphere — rendered live on your phone. Drag to rotate. Let go and it keeps spinning.

The direction: each hex opens into a flat tactical map, a cellular world inside. The sphere becomes the strategic view, each cell a little sandbox of its own. The cells want to be populated with learning agents you can watch, interview, and coach in plain language.

No release date. Built in public, one iteration at a time — stack is Go + Ebitengine, deployed to Android first, desktop later.
